Insights of The Suicide Rates in Asia
(2012 - 2014)


Since the beginning of time, stress is always unavoidable. Whether the stress factors are caused by the death of a loved one, disappointments in life, or injuries/ illnesses, anyone of all ages/ genders/ race can be affected by it. Thus, mental health is equally important as physical health.
In this webpage, we will be looking at different visualizations that aim to provide insights of the suicide rates in Asia from 2012 to 2014.

Choropleth Map Showing The Crude Suicide Rates in Asia

This choropleth map visualizes the severity of the crude suicide rates in Asia from 2012 to 2014. The severity of the crude suicide rates are shown using the red colour luminance. The darker the shade of red , the higher the crude suicide rate in the particular country. and vice versa. From the choropleth map, we know that Japan, Kazakstan, Korea and Mongolia are the top 4 countries with the highest crude suicide rates in Asia from 2012 to 2014.



Line Chart Showing The Trend of The Crude Suicide Rates in Asia

This line chart shows the trend of the median crude suicide rates (per 100k population) of Asia from 2012 to 2014. The median crude suicide rates (per 100k population) of Asia is around 7, but it was gradually decreasing during the 3 years (2012 - 2014), which is visualized in the line chart.



Donut Chart Showing The Percentage Distribution of The Crude Suicide Rates in Asia, Based on Age Groups

The ages can be categorized into 6 categories:
age 5-14 , age 15-24 , age 25-34
age 35-54 , age 55-74 , age 75+

Looking at the donut chart, we know that the age group 75+ has the highest crude suicide rate, which is 30.30%, among the 6 age categories. On the other hand, age group 15-24 has the lowest crude suicide rate, which is 1.15%. This pattern persisted throughout the 3 years, from 2012 to 2014.



Stacked Bar Chart Showing The Difference Between The Average Crude Suicide Rates in Asia, Based on Gender

Looking at the stacked bar chart, the average female crude suicide rates in Asia between 2012 and 2014 were always lower than the average male crude suicide rates.
The difference between the female and male crude suicide rates from 2012 to 2014 ranges around 6.5 to 8 per 100k population.
Also, we can see that the sum of the crude suicide rates of both genders are decreasing every year .
